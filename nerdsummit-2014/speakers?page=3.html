Becky
Sweger
Director of Data & Technology
National Priorities Project

Becky is currently the Director of Data and Technology at National Priorities Project (NPP), a national non-profit organization that was nominated for the 2014 Nobel Peace Prize. She has nearly 20 years of software development experience, with an emphasis on data design and analysis.

At NPP Becky specializes in the localization and publication of open government data for use in interactive tools that make the federal budget accessible to ordinary citizens. She contributes her expertise to organizations like the Sunlight Foundation and the U.S. Treasury Department and can often be found in DC advocating for federal spending transparency.

Before getting involved in open government data, Becky created classroom games and simulations at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania and rolled out a data warehouse at Cooper University Hospital in New Jersey.

Becky has presented around the country and will be on the road again soon to talk data the Participatory Budgeting Project's annual conference. Closer to home, she's an organizer of Hack for Western Mass.
